Data Visualization

Data visualization is the graphical representation of data to understand patterns, trends, distributions, and anomalies. Visual tools such as histograms and box plots help transform raw numerical data into meaningful insights.

Histogram

A histogram represents the frequency distribution of continuous data. Data is grouped into class intervals, and rectangles are drawn whose heights represent frequencies.

Example

Consider marks obtained by students in an exam:

  • Marks: 40, 45, 50, 55, 60, 60, 65, 70, 75, 80

Class intervals of width 10 are formed:

Applications of Histogram

  • Analyzing data distribution
  • Identifying skewness
  • Quality control in manufacturing
  • Performance analysis in engineering systems
