Conditional Probability

Conditional probability deals with the probability of occurrence of an event when another related event has already occurred. It allows us to update the likelihood of an event based on additional information.

This concept plays a crucial role in engineering, data science, machine learning, reliability analysis, and decision-making under uncertainty.

Definition

The conditional probability of an event A given that another event B has occurred is denoted by P(A | B).

This formula indicates that conditional probability is the ratio of the probability of both events occurring together to the probability of the conditioning event.

Interpretation (Intuitive Meaning)

When we say P(A | B), we restrict the sample space to only those outcomes where event B has already occurred. Within this reduced sample space, we then determine the likelihood of event A.

Example 1: Drawing Cards

Two cards are drawn successively from a deck of 52 cards without replacement.

  • Event A: Second card is an Ace
  • Event B: First card is an Ace

Given that the first card is an Ace, the number of remaining cards is 51, out of which 3 are Aces.

P(A | B) = 3 / 51

Example 2: Defective Components

A box contains 10 components, out of which 2 are defective. Two components are selected at random without replacement.

  • Event A: Second component is defective
  • Event B: First component is defective

P(A | B) = 1 / 9

Since one defective component has already been selected, only one defective remains among the remaining 9 components.

Relation Between Conditional Probability and Independence

Two events A and B are said to be independent if the occurrence of one event does not affect the probability of the other.

P(A | B) = P(A)

If this condition holds, then events A and B are independent. Otherwise, they are dependent.

Applications of Conditional Probability

  • Machine learning and Bayesian classifiers
  • Reliability engineering and fault diagnosis
  • Medical diagnosis and testing
  • Network traffic and communication systems
  • Risk assessment and decision making
