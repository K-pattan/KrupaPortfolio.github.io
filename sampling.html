Probability Sampling Methods

Probability sampling is a method of sampling in which every unit of the population has a known and non-zero probability of being selected.

These methods are widely used in statistics because they produce unbiased and reliable results.


Types of Probability Sampling

  • Simple Random Sampling
  • Systematic Sampling
  • Stratified Sampling
  • Cluster Sampling

1. Simple Random Sampling

In Simple Random Sampling, every unit of the population has an equal chance of being selected.

Selection is done using lottery method, random numbers, or computer- generated random values.


Types of Simple Random Sampling


1.1 Sampling With Replacement

In this method, once a unit is selected, it is returned to the population before the next draw. Therefore, the same unit can be selected more than once.

Example:

Suppose there are 5 balls numbered 1 to 5 in a bag. A ball is selected, its number is noted, and then it is put back. This process is repeated.

The same ball may appear again in future selections.

Application:

  • Theoretical probability experiments
  • Simulation studies

1.2 Sampling Without Replacement

In this method, once a unit is selected, it is not returned to the population. Hence, the same unit cannot be selected again.

Example:

From a class of 30 students, 5 students are selected randomly. Once a student is selected, their name is removed from the list.

No student can be selected more than once.

Application:

  • Survey sampling
  • Quality inspection

2. Systematic Sampling

In systematic sampling, every kth unit is selected after choosing a random starting point.

Formula:

k = N / n

Example:

If a population has 1000 units and a sample of 100 is required, then k = 10. Every 10th unit is selected.

3. Stratified Sampling

In stratified sampling, the population is divided into homogeneous groups called strata. Random samples are then taken from each stratum.

Example:

A college population is divided into departments such as CSE, ECE, and ME. Samples are selected separately from each department.

4. Cluster Sampling

In cluster sampling, the population is divided into clusters, and a few clusters are randomly selected. All units within the selected clusters are studied.

Example:

A city is divided into zones. A few zones are selected randomly, and all houses in those zones are surveyed.
